# ENVX2001 — Applied Statistical Methods · Open Handbook

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/1162177395.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19605517)

Welcome to the open handbook for [ENVX2001](https://www.sydney.edu.au/units/ENVX2001), a second-year unit in applied statistics for students in the life and environmental sciences at The University of Sydney.

**[Read the handbook →](https://envx-resources.github.io/ENVX2001-resources/)**

*Authored by Januar Harianto, Si Yang Han, Liana Pozza, and Aaron Greenville at the School of Life and Environmental Sciences, The University of Sydney. Licensed under [CC BY 4.0](LICENSE) and built with [Quarto](https://quarto.org/).*


## Inside the book

ENVX2001 builds on introductory statistics and presents the methods students need for further study and their future careers, using applied examples from agriculture, ecology, and environmental science. The handbook provides:

- **12 weeks of lecture slides** 
- **11 tutorial worksheets**
- **12 lab worksheets**

The content is organised into two modules. *Designed studies* (weeks 1–6) covers sampling, experimental design, and analysis of variance. *Finding patterns in data* (weeks 7–12) covers regression modelling, principal component analysis, clustering, and multidimensional scaling. This is a living resource, updated frequently by staff, students and anyone who wants to contribute.

## Contribute in a pinch

Contributions of every size are welcome and encouraged as this is an Open Educational Resource (OER). Your contributions will help make the material more accessible and useful to students and educators around the world.

How to contribute:

1. [Open an issue](https://github.com/ENVX-resources/ENVX2001-resources/issues/new) — don't be afraid if you are unsure how to do this, start the conversation and we will help you.
2. Send a pull request - to do this you would have made changes to the source code and would like to contribute those changes back to the project. See [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md) for local setup and commit conventions.
